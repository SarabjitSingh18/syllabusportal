Mathematics-I (MTL1001)

Credits: 4 (3-1-0)

Total Marks: 100 | Mid Exam: 30% | End Exam: 70%

Total Lectures: 44

Course Objective

The aim of this course is to introduce key mathematical topics to future engineers, helping them apply mathematical principles to real-life engineering problems. It builds both foundational and advanced understanding for solving engineering-based mathematical problems.

Lecture Breakdown

SECTION A – Matrices (10 Lectures)

  • Introduction to Matrices and Complex matrices (Hermitian, Skew-Hermitian, Unitary, Orthogonal)
  • Rank of a matrix, Gauss–Jordan Method for inverse
  • Normal form, Linear system of equations, Eigen values and Eigen vectors
  • Cayley–Hamilton theorem and its applications, Reduction to diagonal form

SECTION B – Differential Calculus (10 Lectures)

  • Partial derivatives, Homogeneous functions, Euler’s theorem and its extension
  • Total and Composite derivatives, Jacobian, Taylor and Maclaurin expansions
  • Maxima and minima of functions of two variables
  • Lagrange’s method of undetermined multipliers

SECTION C – Integral Calculus (12 Lectures)

  • Double integrals (Cartesian & Polar), Change of order of integration
  • Change of variables and applications of double/triple integrals
  • Beta and Gamma functions

SECTION D – Vector Calculus (12 Lectures)

  • Scalar and Vector point functions, Differentiation of vectors
  • Gradient, Divergence, Curl and Line, Surface & Volume integrals
  • Green’s theorem, Stokes theorem, Gauss divergence theorem (without proofs)

Course Outcomes

  • Ability to calculate matrix rank, characteristic equation, eigenvalues, and apply Cayley-Hamilton theorem.
  • Proficiency in solving calculus problems involving double and triple integrals.
  • Understanding of mathematical techniques used in vector calculus.
